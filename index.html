Hi! I am Audrey Bascoul. I recently graduated with a PhD in Engineering and Project Management (Lean Construction) from UC Berkeley. My research advisor was Professor Iris Tommelein.

Here, you can discover my research interests, ranging from facilities management to structural complexity. On the same page, I have listed my publications to date. During my PhD, I have worked part time for General Contractors and owners. You can learn more about those experiences here. I have also had the chance to be a Teaching Assistant for a semester (CE 167 Engineering and Project Management, and CE 268A: Lean Construction: Principles and Methods), during which I started to develop learning tools. This is a work in progress.

I believe that the Architecture/Engineering/Construction industry is at a turning point due to the commoditization of groundbreaking technologies. In a matter of a few clicks and online trainings, anyone can buy and fly a drone, train a Google API to auto-classify submittals and RFIs, or create digital as-builts on the fly. However, I am convinced that disruption takes more than technology: it takes people and processes.
